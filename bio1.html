PARTS OF A CELL

Before we even talk about parts of a cell, let us first introduce cells. Cells are the basic units of all living things! There are two types of cells: Animal and Plant cells. Here is a basic labelling of the two types of cells.


The three basic parts of an animal cell are nucleus, the cytoplasm, and the cell membrane.

The nucleus is the brain of the cell! It controls the movement of the cell, and it also contains the majority of the cell's genetic material. This genetic material is in the form of DNA.

The cytoplasm is a jelly-like fluid which fills the cell. Organelles are contained in the cytoplasm and are protected by it. More on Organelles will be here.

The cell membrane is a selectively permeable (this will be important when learning diffusion) membrane. It is used to protect the cell from the surroundings, as well as controlling what goes in and out of the cell.



***




This is a plant cell. As you can see, aside from having a nucleus, cytoplasm and a cell membrane, it also contains a vacuole, chloroplasts, and a cell wall.

The vacuole isolates harmful material, and stores food or nutrients for the cell.

The chloroplast is an organelle unique to a plant. It performs photosynthesis, the process of changing water and carbon dioxide into glucose and oxygen. Animals do not photosynthesize and therefore do not have this organelle in their cells.

The cell wall is a layer outside the cell membrane which further protects and supports the cell.
