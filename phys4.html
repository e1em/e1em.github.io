NEWTON'S LAWS

Newton's Laws of Motion are three physical laws that describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it. These are the three laws:

I - Every object in a state of uniform motion tends to remain in that state of motion unless an external force is applied to it.
This is more commonly referred to as the "Law of Inertia".

II - The force is equal to the mass multiplied by the acceleration.
The formula of this law is F = ma.

III - For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.
This means that the forces always come in pairs, and that the opposite forces involved are of the same force.

QUESTIONS!



1. When a rifle shoots out a bullet, is the acceleration of the bullet faster, equal, or slower than the recoiling rifle?

2. When a car bumps into you, is there more force applied on the car or on you?

3. If you rolled a ball on a frictionless surface. Would the ball need force to continue rolling?

4. A force of 50N is applied on a 5 kg block. What is the acceleration of this block?








ANSWERS

1. Faster. Although Newton's Third Law states that the force on the rifle and the bullet are similar. However, the bullet is lighter, and therefore will accelerate faster.

2. Equal Force. Newton's Third Law, everyone!

3. No, it would not. The reason why a ball on earth would stop after you roll it is because there is an opposite friction force acting upon it.

4. 10 m/s2. 50 = 5 x a, and therefore a = 10.
