REPORT: December 10th, 09:55:19

A week-old cheeseburger, by the name of Chi Sburga has been reported missing from his parents, Mr. Bif Sburga and Mrs. Turki Sburga, after casually strolling to a human-owned building with a large double arch sign on its label. After a few minutes inside the store, he went completely out of Mr and Mrs. Sburga’s sight.

Chi Sburga is classified as a W3X Cheeseburger, or for the normal burger citizen, a 10 x 10 x 5 cm cheeseburger with onions and pickles in place of the more popular choice of lettuce.

Not much of the crime from the scene could be recalled by Chi’s parents, but they were kind enough to give some facts regarding his short life. Chi Sburga was created on October 4, 2013. Unlike most burgers, Chi Sburga was able to receive an education, going to the Raffles Chips Institution with other foods from the “double arch store”, referred by the two-legged creatures as “McDonalds”. His favourite classes enjoyed Physical Education and phychology, which were reflected in his hobbies, hiking and learning about human behaviour. He would also often play table tennis at Victoria Park every Sunday, before mysteriously disappearing on December 10, 2015.

Mrs. Turki Sburga noted that when Chi was born, he didn’t have any pickles on him. They grew on him. We are unsure how this is relevant, or if this was an intended pun.

Our main detective for this crime, Big Mack, has brought to us an expert scientist on the human anatomy to help describe this phenomenon, named James Singh.

“I believe that Chi was eaten up by a human and into the mouth,” Singh explained. “The mouth contains teeth and the saliva. “What this means is that Chi is going through the chemical digestion of a human and may come out as a dark brown substance a few days later.”

UPDATE: December 10th, 10:53:20

Our so-called experts from the lab (forgive us, we had a limited budget) believes that by this time, Chi has already arrived the stomach from the oesophagus through the cardiac sphincter for a long time. Here, we define the stomach as a muscular bag, which contains food.

Big Mack has also been able to find one of the many perpetrators from the scene. This perpetrator is by the name of Mr. P. Epsin, a pepsin enzyme. In our latest interview, we were able to extract some important information about how the stomach works. This could potentially help us find the whereabouts of Chi in further detail.

Now to the interview.

BM: Mr. Epsin, can you please tell us what you are? 

P: I’m Pepper Epsin, but known as Mr. P Epsin. I am a protease enzyme, named pepsin. My job is to break down proteins in meat, like burger patties, into shorter chains of amino acids, commonly known as peptides. Burger patties are quite delicious.

BM: Where do you come from?

P: I come from the stomach. More specifically, I am part of the gastric juice formed by the glands in the stomach. You see, the gastric juices not only contain pepsin like me to break down the proteins, but also Hydrochloric Acid. This changes the pH to 2, which is not only awesome for me, as I work best in those contains, but also for the human, since it is good for killing harmful microbes.

BM: This sounds like the chemical digestion in the food. Do you know anything about mechanical digestion? 

P: Of course! We have all experienced it before. *laughs*. To further break up food that come in from the oesophagus, there are muscular contractions known as peristalsis. This process will give us smaller food to digest, which makes our lives much easier.

BM: How long do you think Chi Sburga will stay in this horrible, acidic stomach?

P: I love the acidity! Normally, food will stay in the stomach for 1-4 hours, but since cheeseburgers are created for Americans, he does contain fat and will stay longer. He would probably come out digested, which we would call chyme.

BM: And where will he go?

P: He will leave through the pyloric sphcinter into the duodenum!

BM: Cool. You’re arrested for being a part of the stomach that is trying to break down this innocent cheeseburger, named Chi Sburga.

(P is enzyme-cuffed. A piece of string is stuck onto his mouth and he is no longer able to speak)

Since Big Mack was previously unaware of the pancreas’ existence, he didn’t know where it was. Thankfully, we were also able to find an army of enzymes in the pancreatic juice.

The few regiments include the lipase, pancreatic amylase, maltase, and trypsin. The lipase regiment contains the lipase enzyme that break down the fats and oils into fatty acids and glycerol. The pancreatic amylase will continue breaking down any starch molecules not converted by the salivary amylase into maltose. Then, the maltase on the epithelium cells will break down the maltose into glucose, the remains of the bun. Trypsin is also a regiment, but we didn’t hear a lot about it.

With no way to determine which molecule were part of the cheeseburger, it seems as if Chi Sburga has been broken down and used as fuel for the creature he studied most - the two-legged human.
